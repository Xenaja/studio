common doubts

A few things people worry about for nothing

Custom code is expensive
Not necessarily. Modern development tools speed things up a lot, so a coded site today is comparable in price to a good platform build – and often works out cheaper, because you're not paying a monthly subscription to a website builder.
It takes a long time
A landing page – from two days. I don't spend days hand-building every block: I use a modern approach that gives speed without losing quality or control.
I'll be tied to one developer
Quite the opposite. The code stays yours and any developer can maintain it. And so you can change the content yourself with peace of mind, I build a convenient admin panel – you're not tied to me in any way.
Nothing can be changed without a programmer
You'll be able to. Everything you plan to change regularly – prices, schedule, promos, cards – is surfaced in a simple control panel. Open it, edit, save.

Is custom code really the right call? Let's find out in a minute

A website or a bot can be built on a ready-made website builder or written in custom code. That affects speed, flexibility, uniqueness and how easy it is to make changes later. There's no single right answer – it all depends on your goal. Answer a couple of questions and I'll suggest what's best.

What people usually ask before we begin

What if I don't like the result?
I show the work in stages, not "everything at the end". Edits until you're happy with the version are part of the work, not an extra charge. And before we start we agree on the direction, so we don't head the wrong way.
I don't have a brief and I don't understand the technical side – will it work?
Yes, that's a common situation. I ask questions and help figure out what you actually need. From you – tell me about the business and the task; I'll translate the rest into plain language.
How does payment work?
Simple: 50% upfront, 50% on completion. I name the final price before we start, after a short conversation – with no "along-the-way" surcharges.
What happens after launch?
I hand over the access and, if needed, a simple admin panel – and I stay in touch for support. Small fixes after launch are not a problem.
